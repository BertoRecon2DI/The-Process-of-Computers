The Progress of Computers

Ancient:

The Abacus Is an ancient device used over 5000 years over its creation, mainly to assist in counting a certain number of objects that outnumbered human physical features such as using fingers to count. The abacus like other devices in its time were used to count large numbers in general for various purposes.

Pascal's Calculator Invented byBlaise Pascal,the machine was a mechanical device used as the first adding machine later known as the calculator used between 1642 and 1644 which would give a general idea that would be a stepping stone for later inventions in the future.

Post Modern:

Charles Babbage (1791-1871); Born December 26, 1791 Charles Babbage Was one of four children born to the banker Benjamin Babbage. Charles would study mathematics at Trinity, Cambridge and graduated without honors from Peterhouse in 1814. He was responsible for designing the first automatic computing engines. Invented the computers but failed to build them. The first was made in 2002 in London.

Punch Cards Made by Herman Hollerith , the device is simply a piece of stiff paper that would contain digital information represented by the presence or absence of holes in predefined positions. The information might be data for data processing applications or, in earlier examples, used to directly controlling automated machinery, widely used in the 20th century which is what later became known as the data processing industry, some punch cards are still in use today as to record votes.

Herman Hollerith (1860-1929) Born February 29, 1860, Herman Hollerith was an American Inventor who developed an electromechanical punched card tabulator to assist in summarizing information and later accounting. He would then be the founder of The Tabulating Machine Company that was consolidated in 1911 with three other companies to from the Computing Tabulating-Recording Company. Herman is regarded as one of the seminal figures in the development of data processing which would lead to the improvement and development of future computers.

20th Century

C/C++ The programming language C++(originally named “C” with classes) was devised by Bjarne Stroustrup who was also an employee from Bell Labs (AT&T) in 1979. Was a startup point for programming language that would eventually progress into Java for programming essential point in programming.

Arpanet The precursor of the internet in the past in 1969 with the U.S Defense Department’s Advanced Research Projects Agency Network known as “ARPANET” which had ideally made many of the protocols used for internet communication today.

Steve Jobs(February 24-Oct 5,2011) originally experimented with different pursuits before starting Apple Computer with Steve Wozniak in 1976. One of his first inventions the “blue box” was a simple electronic gizmo that had passed the telephone company billing computers, allowing anyone to make free telephone calls anywhere in the world, despite being illegal for speculations of hacking into a telephone company journal. Eventually sparking passion, a passion to the youth to build electronics and helping in ushering a new era of technology that is computers.

Great Steve Jobs Summary(plus Bill Gates)

Apple A business started by Steve Jobs and Steve Wozniak on April 1, 1976 bringing a new company of changing the way people viewed computers. The company revolves around of creating hardware or software for computers eventually popular products sold worldwide which grew exponentially.

Pong Originally manufactured by Atari as a training exercise later became one of the first video games to be created and serves as a foothold for future games. Acknowledged as one of the earliest arcade videogames and very first sports arcade videogame. Quickly becoming a success and is the first commercially successful video game machine. This would then in turn help establish the video game industry.

Atari The original Atari Inc. founded in 1972 by Nolan Bushnell and Ted Dabney was a pioneer in arcade games. One of its most successful products was the arcade game Pong a simple game of two white bars bouncing a white pixel in between in each other until the pixel would pass a bar scoring a point. Mainly Atari is responsible for helping define the electronic entertainment industry in the 1970’s to the mid-1980’s. Atari is one company that left a foot hold for video games gaining mass popularity worldwide shaping the gaming industry of today
